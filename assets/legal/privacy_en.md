# NapStack Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** 9 April 2026

## 1. Introduction

This privacy policy explains how the **NapStack** mobile app (the “App”) processes data in connection with nap scheduling, alarms, and optional cloud sync. The App is made available by the developer listed as the publisher on **Google Play** (the “**Controller**” or “**we**” / “**us**”).

This document is for information only. If it conflicts with applicable law, the law prevails.

## 2. Data controller

The controller of personal data within the meaning of the GDPR is the entity indicated as the NapStack app developer in **Google Play Console**. You can contact the Controller using the details on the app listing in the store (support email / developer page).

## 3. Data we may process

Depending on which features you use, we may process in particular:

| Category | Description | Examples |
|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Backend account identifier** | Unique user ID assigned on anonymous sign-in with Appwrite | user ID |
| **Nap and schedule data** | Information about planned and completed naps sent for sync | nap type, start and end time, completion status, planned duration |
| **Account preferences** | Auxiliary data linked to your account in the App | e.g. Pro status (cache), purchase-system identifier |
| **Technical and operational data** | Information needed to run the App | e.g. network requests, diagnostic messages at service providers (per their policies) |

We do **not** intentionally collect traditional contact data in the App (e.g. full name, email) unless you use an optional account-linking feature (if offered in the future, it will be described separately).

## 4. Purposes and legal bases (GDPR)

We process data for the following purposes:

1. **Providing the service** — running timers and alarms, saving history, and syncing across devices — **Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR** (contract / use of the service).
2. **Pro purchases** — verifying entitlements, restoring purchases — **Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR**.
3. **Security and stability** — abuse prevention, error diagnostics within a reasonable scope — **Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR** (legitimate interest) or **(b)**, depending on context.

## 5. Recipients and third-party services

To operate the App, we use the following categories of recipients / processors:

- **Appwrite** (backend hosting, database, anonymous authentication, optional server functions) — data stored and processed per project configuration and Appwrite documentation.
- **RevenueCat** — handling and verification of digital purchases (Pro status) — purchase-related user identifiers as required by the SDK integration.
- **Google (Google Play and related services)** — payment fulfilment, App distribution, and possibly Android system services for notifications and alarms.

Each provider’s privacy policy governs how they process data.

## 6. Retention

- We keep backend data **for as long as you use the App** and it is necessary to provide the service, unless longer periods apply by law (e.g. accounting — usually not relevant to nap content).
- After account deletion or on request, we will **delete or anonymise** data to the extent technically feasible and required by law — contact the Controller for details.

## 7. Your rights

You may have the right to:

- **access** your data,
- **rectify** inaccurate data,
- **erase** data (“right to be forgotten”) — within limits set by law,
- **restrict** processing,
- **data portability** (where applicable),
- **object** where the law allows,
- **lodge a complaint** with a supervisory authority (e.g. in Poland: the President of the Personal Data Protection Office).

To exercise your rights, use the contact details on the NapStack listing on Google Play.

## 8. Transfers outside the EEA

Providers such as Appwrite or RevenueCat may process data on servers outside the European Economic Area. Where that happens, mechanisms under the GDPR apply (e.g. standard contractual clauses).

## 9. Notifications and alarms

The App may show **local notifications** and use Android alarm APIs to remind you when a nap ends. These are not marketing messages unless you separately opt in to marketing in the future (which would be described separately).

## 10. Changes

We may update this policy. We will inform you of material changes in a reasonable way (e.g. in-app notice or an updated “last updated” date). Continued use after an update may mean you accept the changes, unless the law requires a different form.
